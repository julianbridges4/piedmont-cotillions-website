Social Dance & Ballroom Etiquette for Young People

The main focus of Piedmont Cotillions is to encourage young people to become confident and to build self esteem.

This program introduces the importance of social graces such as the proper way to move through a receiving line, make introductions and converse comfortably, invite a lady to dance, stand when a lady enters the room, write thank you notes, use proper telephone etiquette, open doors for a lady and much more.

Making friends is very important to young people, but this is not always easy. All young people want to feel accepted, but their shyness and nervousness among their peers may keep them from being socially comfortable. This program helps them present themselves with dignity and shows them how to be at ease with adults and with friends in all social situations.

This is a continuing program and every year it builds on the growth of the previous years. The curriculum changes with the level of maturity. The seventh and eighth graders may review the Foxtrot while adding additional steps. The Cha-cha might be replaced by a new Latin American dance such as the Mambo or the Tango. Of course, each year general etiquette is thoroughly reviewed.

It is our belief that young people who practice proper etiquette from an early age will always feel socially secure, and those who learn to dance will love it for the rest of their lives!

    Geri Sheffey, Instructor and Owner

Geri Sheffey is the owner and instructor of Piedmont Cotillions, LLC. A native of Wethersfield, Connecticut, Geri is a graduate of Bay Path College in Longmeadow, Massachusetts. During her school years, she performed in several Irish tap dancing, ballet, and acrobatic competitions. Geri’s family retired to the Sedgefield community in 1999 after her husband’s 20 years of naval service.

A veteran of the cotillion business for many years, Geri also currently serves on the executive board of the Woman’s Club of Southwest Guilford. She is a past board member of the Millis Road, and JMS PTA, and Sedgefield Swim and Racquet Club. Both of Geri’s children are grown and have gone off to college and careers. She enjoys being an empty nester and volunteering as a study buddy at the elementary schools. Teaching cotillion is Geri’s passion, and it shows in her ability to make the classes enjoyable for her students. She is currently continuing to study ballroom dance and etiquette training under the direction of Beth Stubbs and Fred Astaire dance studio.
